Your Chart as a Story

Here is the story your chart is telling. Seven chapters. Read it once, then watch it play out in the months above.

The first thing to understand about your chart is your Rising sign — called the Lagna in Vedic astrology. It's the sign that was on the eastern horizon the exact moment you were born at 9:47 PM on March 2, 1999. This sign shapes your physical body, how you arrive in a room, and the lens through which every planet in your chart expresses itself.

Yours is Virgo — specifically in a nakshatra called Hasta. Nakshatras are 27 "zoom lens" divisions inside the zodiac signs — more precise than just the sign alone. Hasta means "the hand." Moon-ruled. The healing hand, the skilled and precise hand, the hand that shapes and restores. People with Hasta Rising carry a quality of careful attentiveness and healing skill that operates through direct presence and service — your body carries it even before you speak.

✦ What is Vedic Astrology? ▼ more

Astrology is a system built on the idea that the sky at the exact moment of your birth carries meaningful information about who you are and the life you came here to live. Astrologers map planet positions against the zodiac — a symbolic wheel of 12 signs — and interpret how those positions shape personality, relationships, timing, and soul direction.

Vedic astrology (Jyotish) is the system used in this planner. It's the traditional astrology of India, thousands of years old. It uses the sidereal zodiac — anchored to the actual positions of the fixed stars in the sky. This is different from Western astrology, which uses the tropical zodiac, anchored to the seasons.

The two systems use the same planet names and sign names, but their calculations differ by about 23.9° (called the Ayanamsha). This is why your Vedic Rising sign (Virgo) differs from your Western Rising sign (Libra) — same sky, different measurement system. Neither is wrong.

This planner uses Lahiri Ayanamsha — the most widely used Vedic calculation, recognized by the Indian government — and Whole Sign Houses, the oldest house system in Vedic astrology.

✦ In your planner: All planet positions in the sidebar are sidereal (Vedic). The Western Big Three section shows your tropical positions for comparison. The lunation dates use tropical sign names but Vedic house assignments from your Virgo Rising.
↑ Rising Sign · The Lagna ▼ more

The Rising sign (Lagna) is the zodiac sign that was on the eastern horizon at the exact moment and place you were born. Because the Earth rotates, a new sign rises every ~2 hours — which is why birth time matters so much. It's not your "personality" the way a Sun sign is. It's more like the body and lens your soul chose for this lifetime. Everything in the chart is colored by it.

In Vedic astrology, the Rising sign is considered the most important factor in the chart — even more important than the Sun sign. It determines your house system, your chart ruler, and your overall physical constitution and life navigation.
